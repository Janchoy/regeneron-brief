Context
Where Regeneron Stands in 2026

Regeneron enters 2026 with a great deal working in its favor. Dupixent has become one of the most successful drugs in immunology — $17.8B in 2025 global sales, up 26% year-over-year1, with pediatric CSU approved in April 2026 and additional indications in the pipeline.18 The Regeneron Genetics Center has sequenced over three million exomes and is building what may become the world's largest DNA-linked healthcare database.4 The pipeline spans 45 candidates across six therapeutic areas.2 The scientific engine is running well.

At the same time, the commercial picture is shifting. EYLEA — the ophthalmology franchise that anchored Regeneron's commercial scale for over a decade — is under growing biosimilar pressure. Amgen's biosimilar Pavblu entered market in October 2024 as the first and currently only marketed competitor.5 Five additional biosimilars — from Sandoz, Teva/Alvotech, Biocon, Formycon, and Celltrion — have all settled patent disputes with Regeneron and are permitted to launch by Q4 2026 or December 31, 2026.6 Combined EYLEA and EYLEA HD US sales were down 28% in Q3 2025.5 A manufacturing setback at a third-party facility added complexity.7 The company has committed over $7 billion to expanding US manufacturing capacity8 — a thoughtful long-term investment that takes time to bear out.

Against this backdrop, Divya Pathak joined as Regeneron's inaugural Chief AI Officer in February 20269, and the Senior Director, AI Strategy & Operations role was established to help her build the organization. The work is meaningful: AI has the potential to accelerate the pipeline, support the commercial transition, and scale responsibly across a company that takes its science seriously.

Pipeline
Where AI Can Support the Science

Regeneron's pipeline is where the long-term growth story lives. The programs advancing in 2026 span immunology, oncology, gene therapy, cardiovascular, and obesity — with total addressable markets projected to exceed $220 billion by 2030.13 In each area, there are thoughtful ways for AI to contribute — from accelerating enrollment to supporting target discovery to making regulatory submissions more rigorous.

AI Opportunity
Where the Work Is and What's Needed to Do It Well

Regeneron has meaningful AI infrastructure already in place. The RGC platform, the enterprise GenAI deployment, and the Weill Cornell clinical AI collaboration are real starting points. The question is less whether AI can add value — it clearly can — and more about sequencing, governance, and building the operating model that allows it to scale responsibly.

RGC Genomics Platform
Drug Discovery · High Readiness
The Regeneron Genetics Center has sequenced 3M+ exomes linked to EHR data on Databricks/AWS, already achieving 600x query speed improvement.11 AI-driven target identification sits at the top of Regeneron's drug discovery funnel — extending this to multi-omic analytics and antibody optimization for the VelociSuite pipeline builds directly on infrastructure already in place.
P1Infrastructure in place
Enterprise GenAI Platform
Productivity · Live & Scaling
Thousands of Regeneron employees already use natural language querying against Regeneron-specific data.15 Deepening integration — RAG on internal scientific literature, clinical trial data, and RGC findings — and eventually agentic workflows for research synthesis, represents the natural next step for a platform already in users' hands.
P1Platform live · expand depth
Clinical Trial Stratification
R&D Efficiency · High Value
Weill Cornell collaboration published AI-driven patient stratification methodology in Nature Communications.16 In April 2026, Regeneron partnered with TriNetX to access de-identified EHR data covering 300 million patients.19 With 18 Phase III programs in 2026 and oncology enrollment times exceeding 25 months,10 the data foundation for AI-driven patient selection is strengthening.
P2Foundation strengthening
Manufacturing Quality AI
Operations · Pressing
The Novo Indiana facility setback exposed third-party manufacturing risk.7 Regeneron has committed $7B+ to new US facilities in Tarrytown, Rensselaer, and North Carolina.8 AI for anomaly detection and quality monitoring in fill/finish operations is directly relevant to the new facility build and EYLEA HD commercial continuity.
P2Relevant to new facility build
Commercial Analytics
Revenue · Near-Term
Dupixent received FDA approval for pediatric CSU in April 202618 and continues to expand across indications. AI-driven HCP targeting and patient identification for new indication launches maps directly to commercial execution and is grounded in data Regeneron already holds.
P2Commercial pull · data in-house
Agentic AI Governance
Frontier · Forward Planning
Most enterprise AI governance frameworks were designed for LLM tools — human-initiated, human-reviewed. Agentic systems introduce different failure modes: drift, cycles, missing context — silent failures that don't trigger errors.17 Building governance infrastructure for agentic trajectory monitoring is the next frontier for any organization deploying AI in high-stakes scientific and manufacturing contexts.

The Role
What the Senior Director, AI Strategy & Operations Is Being Asked to Do

The job description describes the Senior Director as "the senior strategic and operational partner to the SVP, Chief AI Officer, operating with broad latitude and minimal direction." The role enables enterprise-wide AI strategy execution, ensures cross-functional alignment, and drives operational excellence across the AI organization. The role is less about having all the answers and more about creating the conditions — portfolio clarity, governance infrastructure, cross-functional trust — that allow the AI organization to do its best work.

01
AI Strategy Support
Support the CAIO in shaping and operationalizing Regeneron's enterprise-wide AI strategy and associated strategic priorities. Support execution of the CAIO's strategic and operational agenda.
02
Portfolio & Planning
Maintain oversight of the enterprise AI portfolio and roadmap. Partner with the CAIO on multi-year planning, prioritization, and portfolio oversight. Monitor metrics and prepare executive-level materials to support decision-making.
03
Governance Contribution
Contribute to establishing and maturing the central AI and Data Governance model. Assess opportunities, risks, and dependencies that influence enterprise decision-making.
04
Operating Model Design
Develop frameworks, standards, and operating models for AI programs, platforms, and investments.
05
Cross-Functional Partnership
Partner across business, scientific, and technology functions to support alignment, integration, and execution of AI initiatives. Build trusted relationships across domains and coordinate preparation for executive meetings, steering committees, and board forums.
